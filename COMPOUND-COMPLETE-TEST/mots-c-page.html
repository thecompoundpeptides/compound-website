Mitochondrial peptide that enhances metabolism, exercise performance, and cellular energy

Overview

MOTS-c (Mitochondrial Open Reading Frame of the 12S rRNA-c) is a revolutionary mitochondrial-derived peptide that acts as an exercise mimetic. This 16-amino acid peptide regulates metabolic functions throughout the body, improving insulin sensitivity, enhancing fat metabolism, and increasing exercise capacity.

Discovered in 2015, MOTS-c has been called the "exercise in a bottle" peptide due to its ability to activate pathways typically triggered by physical exercise, making it valuable for metabolic optimization and athletic performance.
