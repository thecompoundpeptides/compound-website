Glutathione

The body's master antioxidant for detoxification, immune support, and cellular protection

Overview

Glutathione is a tripeptide composed of glutamine, cysteine, and glycine that serves as the body's most powerful antioxidant and detoxification agent. Often called the "master antioxidant," glutathione is essential for maintaining cellular health, supporting immune function, and protecting against oxidative stress.

Found in every cell of the body, glutathione levels naturally decline with age, stress, poor diet, and environmental toxins. Supplementation can restore optimal levels, leading to improved energy, clearer skin, better cognitive function, and enhanced overall health. It's particularly crucial for liver detoxification and protecting cells from damage.

How It Works

Mechanism of Action

Glutathione works through multiple pathways:

  • Direct Antioxidant: Neutralizes reactive oxygen species and free radicals
  • Enzyme Cofactor: Essential for glutathione peroxidase and transferase
  • Detoxification: Binds to toxins for elimination via liver and kidneys
  • Immune Modulation: Regulates T-cell function and cytokine production
  • DNA Synthesis: Required for DNA synthesis and repair
  • Regeneration: Recycles other antioxidants like vitamins C and E

Why Supplementation?

Oral glutathione has poor bioavailability. Injectable forms bypass digestion, delivering glutathione directly to cells where it's needed most. Liposomal forms also offer enhanced absorption.

Safety & Side Effects

Glutathione has an excellent safety profile as a naturally occurring substance:

Generally Well-Tolerated

  • Mild injection site discomfort
  • Temporary skin flushing
  • Rare allergic reactions
  • Mild nausea (high doses)

Optimization Tips

  • Combine with N-acetylcysteine (NAC) for production support
  • Take with alpha-lipoic acid for recycling
  • Ensure adequate selenium intake
  • Support with B vitamins
  • Avoid alcohol during treatment

Contraindications

  • Asthma (use with caution)
  • Sulfur sensitivity
  • During chemotherapy (consult oncologist)
  • Pregnancy (limited safety data)
