How It Works

Mechanism of Action

Melanotan II works through melanocortin receptor activation:

  • MC1R Activation: Stimulates melanin production in skin cells
  • MC3R/MC4R: Affects appetite and metabolism
  • MC4R in CNS: Enhances sexual arousal and function
  • Systemic Effects: Influences multiple body systems
  • Long Half-Life: Effects last days after injection

Tanning Process

MT2 stimulates melanogenesis (melanin production) even without UV exposure, though minimal sun exposure enhances results. The tan develops gradually over 1-2 weeks and can last months with maintenance dosing.

Dosing Protocol

Important Note

Start with very low doses to assess tolerance. Nausea is common initially but typically subsides.

Administration Tips

  • Inject subcutaneously, preferably before bed to minimize nausea
  • Stay hydrated - MT2 can cause flushing
  • Start low and increase gradually
  • Some UV exposure enhances tanning (10-15 minutes)
  • Effects are cumulative - be patient

Safety & Side Effects

Common Side Effects

  • Nausea (especially initially)
  • Facial flushing
  • Decreased appetite
  • Spontaneous erections (men)
  • Darkening of moles/freckles
  • Fatigue after injection

Important Precautions

  • Monitor moles for changes
  • Start with very low doses
  • Not recommended for those with melanoma history
  • May affect blood pressure
  • Can cause temporary stomach upset

Contraindications

  • History of melanoma or suspicious moles
  • Cardiovascular disease
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Uncontrolled blood pressure
